# marketing-landing-expressive-branded-bike-share (coverage brief, Codex-authored)

title: Neighborhood Bike Share Launch
register: marketing/landing
aestheticStyle: expressive-branded
audience: Urban commuters, students, and visitors considering short local trips.
goal: Persuade first-time riders to try a new neighborhood bike share service.
requiredContent:
- Service name: LoopLane
- Short value proposition
- How it works in three steps
- Membership and pay-per-ride options
- Coverage area summary
- Safety and support information
- Primary sign-up action
constraints:
- audience: Assume users may be unfamiliar with bike share programs.
- brand-tone-in-words: Energetic, local, practical, and welcoming.
- platform: Responsive public marketing page for mobile and desktop web.
- baseline: Must meet common accessibility expectations and make key information understandable without external assets.
successCriteria:
- Visitors quickly understand what the service is and why they might use it.
- Pricing, availability, and sign-up path are easy to find.
- The experience feels credible for both everyday commuters and occasional riders.
