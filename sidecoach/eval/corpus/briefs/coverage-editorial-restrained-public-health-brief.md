# editorial-restrained-public-health-brief (coverage brief, Codex-authored)

title: Public Health Research Explainer
register: editorial
aestheticStyle: restrained
audience: General readers seeking a careful explanation of new public health research.
goal: Explain a fictional study’s findings, limits, and practical implications without overstating certainty.
requiredContent:
- Article title
- Short summary
- Study context
- Key findings
- What the study does not prove
- Expert commentary from fictional sources
- Practical takeaways
- Links section with placeholder source descriptions
constraints:
- audience: Readers may have varied scientific literacy and may be sensitive to health claims.
- brand-tone-in-words: Careful, balanced, plain, and responsible.
- platform: Responsive news or public-interest article page.
- baseline: Must be accessible and clearly distinguish evidence from interpretation.
successCriteria:
- Readers understand the finding and its limits.
- The article avoids alarm, hype, or unsupported advice.
- Important caveats are visible and understandable.
