# PRODUCT.md - claude-dotfiles marketing microsite

## Register

**Brand-side.** This is a marketing site for an opinionated open-source dev tool. The product is technical infrastructure (CLAUDE.md rules, hooks, skills, memory subsystem) but the surface is the brand. Lean editorial / engineering-firm, not SaaS-product.

## Who this is for

**Primary audience:** Senior developers and tech leads evaluating AI workflow tooling. They've used Claude Code, they know what discipline problems they hit, they're skeptical of "AI workflow" hype, and they will judge the site by how *unhyped* it looks.

**Secondary audience:** Engineering managers looking for team consistency tooling - the "our six devs all run Claude differently" problem.

**Tertiary audience:** Design-engineering hybrid folks who care about polish and will dock points if the marketing site itself looks generic.

## Brand personality

Three physical-object words: **engineering-firm tailor**, **quiet midnight workshop**, **annotated editorial**.

Confident, opinionated, considered, quiet. Editorial - not corporate. Mature, not loud. The voice of someone who's been doing this work for years and has earned the right to be specific.

NOT: hyped, salesy, AI-buzz, dev-tool-generic, startup-pastels, "10x your productivity," abstract 3D Vercel-aesthetic, animated mesh-gradient hero.

## Anti-references

Avoid the saturated landing-page lanes:

- **Vercel / Linear monochrome-gradient hero.** Overdone; reads as default.
- **SaaS template with abstract 3D shapes and a "Get started for free" CTA.** Wrong category.
- **AI-product hype** (purple-and-cyan gradient, "10x your productivity," product screenshot with annotation arrows). Anti-everything we are.
- **Klim-influenced editorial-typographic** (display-serif italic + small mono labels + ruled separators + monochromatic restraint). Saturated lane per Impeccable's reflex-reject - the brief here doesn't *require* it.
- **Dev-tool-generic.** Inter + dark background + neon accent + monospace code blocks. The default for this category. Reject.

## Strategic principles

1. **Discipline before sparkle.** The product is a system of constraints. The site reflects that with structural rigor, not motion theatre.
2. **Confident quietness over loud claims.** Yes& isn't selling a SaaS - it's selling a method. Methods are explained, not exclaimed.
3. **Show, don't shout.** The proof IS that the dotfiles work. Numbered components, real hook names, real commands. No abstract claims like "more productive."
4. **Respect the reader's time.** A senior dev evaluating tools wants the *substance*, not the marketing dance. Above-the-fold should land the bet in one paragraph.
5. **The brand mark earns its place.** The red ampersand is the signature gesture. Used sparingly - it should feel deliberate, not pasted on every section.

## Brand visual references

The Yes& wordmark itself is a humanist transitional serif with high contrast (thin verticals / thick bowls) - reads as editorial / publishing house. The ampersand is calligraphic, ornate, almost like a hand-set initial. Brand red is a saturated cherry/tomato red (`#DC2618`-ish), not orange-leaning. Dark color is an off-black with a slight green undertone (`#1A1F1B`-ish). Light background is a warm cream (`#F4EFE4`-ish), not pure white.

Three brand-voice typography decisions:
- **Display serif** for headings - matches the wordmark's editorial character. Source Serif 4 (Adobe, OFL).
- **Humanist grotesque** for body - structural contrast against the serif, readable at small sizes. Hanken Grotesk (OFL).
- **Mono** for code - JetBrains Mono.

All three are off the `fontshare-reference` reflex-reject list. Inter, Fraunces, Outfit, Instrument Serif explicitly *not used*.
