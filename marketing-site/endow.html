visual / endow

Endow the UI. Claude builds the scene.

endow is the microadjustment toolbar for Claude Code. Queue a change through prompt mode or manipulate mode; Claude works the scene in the background and refreshes when it's done.

What it does

Two modes. One queue. One scene.

endow is a toolbar that sits over your dev server. You select an element, make an offer in whichever mode fits the thought, and the queue builds. When you hand it off, Claude works through the whole stack at once and lands the scene with a refresh.

Prompt mode.

Select an element. Type the offer in plain English. "Heavier heading," "tighten this card's padding," "swap the border for a shadow." Each prompt is an offer in the queue, scoped to the element you picked.

Good for the thoughts that are easier to say than to dial in - directional notes, vibe shifts, the kind of thing you'd whisper to a designer over their shoulder.

Manipulate mode.

Select an element. Reach for the exact property you want to endow it with. Font weight, color, radius, spacing, shadow, text alignment. Every nudge becomes an offer in the queue.

Good for the thoughts that are easier to point at than to describe - "this much padding, this color, this radius." The toolbar reads your DESIGN.md tokens and surfaces them as the options.

Queue, then scene.

Offers stack as you go. Stack five, stack twenty - the toolbar holds them all. When you're ready, hand them to Claude in one pass.

Claude works the offers in the background, through your real source code, through your real build pipeline. The browser refreshes when the scene is done. No half-applied state, no round-trip per tweak, no "let me regenerate."

How to start

One install. Then start endowing.

endow is one component of the dotfiles. Install it standalone or as part of the full toolkit. 

./install.sh --only endow

The installer wires the toolbar, the MCP server, and the queue. Open a project, run endow start, and the toolbar mounts over your dev server.

Two heightenings on the way.

endow's first beat is the queue. Two more, both rooted in improv principles, are on the roadmap.

Offers mode.

Before you commit to a direction, ask Claude for variations on a single offer. Three takes laid side by side - typography one way, motion another, color a third. Pick the one that lands the scene; the others fall away.

For the moments when you know the change is needed but don't yet know what it should be.

Sidecoach integration.

Stack offers using sidecoach verbs instead of freeform prompts. /bolder on this card, /quieter on this section, /polish on this whole region - flow-oriented tasking with the full sidecoach pipeline (PRODUCT.md context, DESIGN.md tokens, QA gate triad) behind every offer.

For the moments when the change is part of a phase, not a one-off tweak.
